About Amy & Mike

The FounderAmy Puzey

Amy Puzey is a researcher and the founder and CEO of Goodphyte, the company that brought phytase to human health. She is the visionary translating decades of phytase science into a way to improve human health.

Amy's life's purpose is closing the nutritional gap that phytic acid creates in the standard diet. Plants evolved phytic acid to bind essential minerals in seeds so the germinating plants would have everything they needed. Unfortunately, humans don't make much of the phytase needed to break down phytic acid and absorb the minerals and amino acids found in the grains, legumes, nuts, and seeds at the base of much of what we eat. This nutritional gap is a hidden driver of fatigue, inflammation, autoimmunity, and chronic disease.

Amy discovered the solution to closing that gap: supplementing phytase, the enzyme that unlocks the iron, copper, zinc, magnesium, calcium and amino acids bound up by phytic acid and frees them so people can absorb them. She founded Goodphyte in 2019 to bring that solution to the world. The problem she is solving is known as "hidden hunger" — micronutrient deficiency that persists even when food is abundant.

Amy has a background in nursing from the University of Alberta and worked as a nutritionist in private practice for nine years before turning her focus to phytase.

She is also an accomplished endurance athlete. Amy has won multiple single-day and multi-day ultra races, won the Canadian Mountain Running Championship, and represented Canada twice at the international level. Before Goodphyte she led 5 Peaks Adventures, Canada's national trail-running series, for nine years as CEO and National Race Director.

The ScientistDr. Mike Bedford

Dr. Mike Bedford is widely recognized as the world's foremost authority on the phytase enzyme — the peer-reviewed science behind Goodphyte.

Over a research career spanning four decades and nearly 300 peer-reviewed papers, Dr. Bedford established much of the mechanistic understanding of how phytic acid (also called phytate) locks up the minerals in plant foods, and how phytase breaks it down to release them. His work drove the commercialization of phytase in animal nutrition and its translation into human health.

In 2019 that science crossed into human nutrition when Amy Puzey and Goodphyte became the first to bring phytase to market for human health, drawing directly on the evidence base Dr. Bedford's research helped build. He serves as Goodphyte's scientific advisor.

Dr. Bedford is a compelling guest for science-heavy podcasts where peer-reviewed credentials and mechanistic depth matter — from the chemistry of the phytate molecule to superdosing, gut immunity, and why four decades of livestock research translate directly to human mineral nutrition. He appears alongside Amy Puzey as a researcher-and-founder pairing.
